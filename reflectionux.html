Reflection

Upon my initial introduction to the graphic and web design studio, I was brimming with excitement to learn new things and eagerly anticipated the opportunity to innovate a website. At the beginning of week one, I perused the blackboard syllabus and familiarised myself with the basics of HTML/CSS. I took it upon myself to independently learn coding and hoped that I would be able to adapt to the course content in the following weeks. Despite my worries, I decided to manage my time effectively and allocated ample time towards learning the course material and utilizing the resources provided on the blackboard.

In retrospect, Looking back, I realized that by completing my worksheets on time, I didn't encounter any problems. This motivated me to enhance the skills I learned from workshops. To achieve this, I started researching and studying on W3school (W3Schools, 2023). My goal was to create something that is professionally made, as if it was intended for a client or consumers.

While wireframing, I aimed to incorporate a contemporary design and personalize it with my own unique touch. I created multiple wireframe pages, some of which were themed. I explored different methods of site navigation and ensured that all designs were compatible with Flexbox, mobile devices, responsive screen sizes, and other cross-platforms.

In hindsight, I have noticed significant improvements in both my creative abilities and technical skills in coding over the past few weeks. As part of a project involving posters and typography systems, I used Adobe InDesign and Adobe Illustrator, which helped me develop my skills in using these creative software. I felt more confident and self-sufficient in completing my posters and typography tasks.

While working on a project, I faced difficulties with designing posters and typography. I needed assistance in understanding the concepts and decided to read the handbook of the course non-design book by Robin Williams (Williams,R. 2008). This helped me to study the concepts of contrast, repetition, alignment, and proximity, which in turn guided me with the C.R.A.P task. Once I gained a good understanding of what to do for creating my poster, I searched for inspiration and found a poster that caught my eye (Zazzle, 2023). I then created three versions of my poster using this inspiration. I have themed my posters with California style and have further explained it in my C.R.A.P task on each poster.

For my typography task, I conducted individual research online to explore the eight typographic systems. During my research, I found a detailed blog post that explains how each system works (Visualdesignfordh, 2014). After accumulating the knowledge, I began designing my own typographic systems. To maintain consistency, I used black, white, and grey as my color palette. Though it was my first time creating typographic systems, I enjoyed the process and spent many hours perfecting my work. Gradually, I became more efficient with each system.

Coding the site was straightforward since I had already planned and wireframed most of its functions and design elements. During the coding process, I received feedback from Gigi who suggested using animated styles CSS (Eden, D. 2023). This helped me create flashy text for my headers and images. I tried to incorporate everything I learned into the site and even did additional work to enhance the user experience. I will include all the resources I used for the site in my reference list for submission.

In conclusion, I enjoyed the process and was quite happy with the outcome. However, I can improve with time by learning more new languages and improving my Javascript.
